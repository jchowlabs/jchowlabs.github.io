ID Verification in the AI Era

Traditional identity verification relies on onboarding and recovery workflows that assume human presence and authenticity. These mechanisms are often manual or semi-automated, triggered during first-time access, account recovery, or high-risk actions, and typically include knowledge-based checks, document verification, biometric capture, or live interaction. While effective in the past, many of these approaches break down in the AI era.

Advances in AI have enabled AI-driven voice and video impersonation, as well as automated interactions, making it possible to convincingly spoof legitimate users across a wide range of identity verification workflows. This guide examines real-world attack techniques and the common failure patterns that allow onboarding and recovery processes to be bypassed in practice. It also outlines design principles and capabilities that help organizations build more resilient identity verification strategies in the face of evolving AI-enabled threats.
